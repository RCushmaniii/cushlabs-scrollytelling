Here's the problem.

Investors see hundreds of pitch decks every month. Most of them are twelve slides in a Google Doc that look exactly like the twelve slides they saw yesterday.

Ninety-two percent of investors say storytelling influences their decision more than data alone. Yet most founders hand them a spreadsheet with a logo on it.

You have five seconds before a pitch is judged. Five seconds to earn the next five minutes.

And interactive narratives generate three times more engagement than traditional slide decks. Three times.

So the question isn't whether your story matters. It's whether your delivery does justice to it.
